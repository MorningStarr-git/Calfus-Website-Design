Typography Rules

Space Grotesk is display-only

H1 through H2 are always Space Grotesk 700. H3 and below are DM Sans. They never occupy the same level or compete.

Tight tracking at display size is mandatory

Hero headline must use letter-spacing: -3px to -4px. Loose tracking at 108px looks amateur. This is non-negotiable.

Lora is controlled and intentional

Testimonial block only. Pull quotes only. Never more than one or two instances per page. Do not use Lora for headings.

Body copy minimum 16px everywhere

Editorial moments (problem section, testimonial) go 17–18px DM Sans 300 weight. Bigger body signals confidence in the writing.

Never centre body text or lists

Left-align all paragraphs and lists. Centre only hero headlines and the testimonial quote. This is a hard rule.

The italic teal "it." in the hero is sacred

Space Grotesk 700 italic on it. in teal colour. This references "Run with it." without spelling it out. Do not change the colour, weight, or style.

5–6px teal circle before every overline label. Consistent across all sections. Never skip this.

Footer dot

Animated teal dot between footer brand elements. Same 3s pulse as logo dot. Signals "still alive" in the footer.

13 — Motion

Motion System

Every animation has a job. Nothing moves for decoration. If removing an animation makes the page worse to understand — it stays. If it just "looks cool" — it goes.

Duration Scale
Instant
100ms
Hover colour changes, active states, cursor
Fast
200ms
Button states, icon swaps, toggles
Medium
300ms
Card hovers, dropdowns, tooltips
Slow
500ms
Section entrances, modal
XSlow
800ms
Hero animations, large visual transitions
Easing Curves
Entrance
cubic-bezier(0.0, 0.0, 0.2, 1)
Fast start, gentle settle. Elements coming in — hero cards, section reveals, modal appears. Feels like things arrive with purpose.
Exit
cubic-bezier(0.4, 0.0, 1, 1)
Quick and clean. Elements leaving — modal dismiss, dropdown close, page transitions. Never lingers. Gets out of the way.
Scroll Reveal Rules

Fade up from 24px

All scroll-triggered elements: opacity: 0 → 1 and translateY(24px → 0). Duration 600ms, entrance easing.

Trigger at 8% viewport

IntersectionObserver threshold 0.08. Element begins animating when 8% of it is in view. Not too early, not too late.

Stagger siblings at 80ms

Grid children stagger: d1=80ms, d2=160ms, d3=240ms, d4=320ms delay. Groups feel like they arrive together, not all at once.

No parallax. Ever.

The spec is explicit: no parallax. It's decorative, hurts performance, causes motion sickness, and is impossible to make accessible.

No auto-playing carousels

If content moves on its own without user intent — remove it. The marquee is the one exception because it is purely additive ticker content, not primary content.

14 — Do / Don't

Usage Rules

The most important rules for keeping the system coherent as the team expands it.

✓ DO

Use Teal only on interactive elements — buttons, CTAs, active states, the cursor, and data highlights. It earns its meaning.
Use Space Grotesk 700 for all H1–H2 display headings. This is the only acceptable heading font.
Lead with outcomes in copy — "deployed in 2.3s" not "we have a fast deployment pipeline".
Maintain 96px section padding top and bottom on all sections. Never reduce.
Use the dot — in overlines, on the logo, as accent in stats, on hover states. It's the most ownable brand element.
Left-align all body text and lists. Centre only hero headlines and the testimonial quote.
Use DM Sans 300 weight for body copy. 400 for UI. 500 for labels and buttons.
Apply scroll reveals on all content sections. Never let things just "be there" without a moment of arrival.

✕ DON'T

Use Teal as a background colour, texture, or decorative fill. Teal belongs to actions and the dot only.
Use Violet on buttons. Violet is for intelligence/platform moments — not conversion actions.
Use Amber on body text or buttons. It is a data/proof accent colour. One context only.
Mix Space Grotesk with any other serif or display typeface on the same page. No exceptions.
Add parallax effects. The spec is explicit. They're decorative, bad for performance, and inaccessible.
Use Coral more than once or twice per page. Its rarity is the source of its impact.
Centre paragraphs, bullet lists, or body copy blocks. Centre is for headlines and quotes only.
Change the #0F0E2A deep violet-black to navy. Navy reads "IT services 2018." The violet-black is deliberate.
